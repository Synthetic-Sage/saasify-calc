How to Calculate Software ROI in B2B

Return on Investment (ROI) is the ultimate metric for software procurement. When purchasing a B2B SaaS product, the tool must either directly generate revenue or save the company significant operational costs. Our calculator focuses on the latter: efficiency gains.

The Formula

To accurately calculate 1st-year ROI, you must account for all upfront costs and compare them to the financial value of the time saved by your engineering or operational team.

ROI = ((Total Value Created - Total Costs) / Total Costs) × 100
  • ▶
    Total Costs (1st Year): (Monthly SaaS Fee × 12) + One-time Setup & Training Cost.
  • ▶
    Total Value Created (1st Year): (Hours Saved per Month × Hourly Wage) × 12.

Why Setup Costs Matter

A common mistake in B2B procurement is ignoring implementation costs. A tool might cost $500/month, but if it takes 3 engineers an entire week to migrate data and learn the system, that is a hidden cost of thousands of dollars that severely impacts your 1st-year ROI.
