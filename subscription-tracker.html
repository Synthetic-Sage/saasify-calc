The Hidden Cost of SaaS Sprawl

The shift to cloud computing revolutionized the way companies operate, moving software procurement from painful, multi-year, on-premise contracts to frictionless monthly subscriptions. However, this lack of friction has created a new enterprise epidemic: SaaS Sprawl.

What is SaaS Sprawl?

SaaS Sprawl occurs when different departments, teams, or even individual employees purchase software subscriptions independently, without central IT oversight. The result is a fragmented technology stack filled with redundant applications and forgotten subscriptions.

  • ▶
    Redundancy: The Marketing team buys Asana, while Engineering uses Jira, and Operations uses Trello. You are paying three different companies to solve the same project management problem.
  • ▶
    Orphaned Accounts: When an employee leaves the company, their email is deactivated, but their $50/month Salesforce seat is never unassigned.
  • ▶
    Shadow IT: Employees bypass procurement using company credit cards, creating major security vulnerabilities and unmapped costs.

The 30% Waste Rule

Industry analysts estimate that up to 30% of a company's total SaaS spend is completely wasted on unused licenses. If your organization spends $500,000 annually on software, $150,000 of that is likely evaporating into the cloud without generating any business value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can we prevent SaaS bloat?
Implement a centralized SaaS management platform (SMP), mandate single sign-on (SSO) for all apps to easily track logins, and conduct quarterly audits of active software licenses.
What is considered a normal SaaS budget?
While it varies by industry, modern technology companies often spend between $2,000 to $4,000 per employee annually on SaaS subscriptions.
